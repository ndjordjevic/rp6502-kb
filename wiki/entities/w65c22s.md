# W65C22S

**Summary**: The WDC CMOS 65C22 VIA. Slot **U5** on [[rp6502-board]]. Memory-mapped at `$FFD0-$FFDF` — 16 bytes of classic 6522 register file. Like the CPU, no substitution allowed.

---

## Role

Gives 6502 programmers the familiar 6522 interface — two 8-bit ports, two 16-bit timers, shift register, handshaking — in front of the **J1 GPIO** expansion header on [[rp6502-board]].

See [[6522-via]] for the full register reference (Port A/B, DDR, timers T1/T2, Shift Register, PCR, ACR, IFR, IER) and typical init sequence.

## Address

`$FFD0-$FFDF` (16 bytes). The OS and RIA never touch it, so it behaves exactly as a bare 6522 in a classic build.

## Expansion

The `$FF00-$FFCF` gap above the VIA is **unassigned** and reserved for hobbyists to wire additional VIAs, SID chips, etc. Example layout from [[rp6502-os-docs]]: VIA0 at `$FFD0`, VIA1 at `$FFC0`, SID0 at `$FF00`, SID1 at `$FF20`.

## Hardware substitution

- Must be genuine WDC **W65C22S** (part `W65C22S6TPG-14`).
- No NMOS 6522s. No out-of-production CMOS variants expected to work long-term.
