# yt-ep11 — No Soldering and No Breadboard

**Summary**: Demonstrates building a Picocomputer from PCBWay-assembled boards with zero soldering: firmware loading, IC insertion, acrylic case assembly, and a board giveaway announcement.

---

## Key topics

- **PCBWay sponsorship**: single-unit assembly for $30; two units at $15 each. First demonstration that the reference design is production-manufacturable as a single unit.
- **Open source manufacturing**: all design files publicly released; anyone can send to any PCB manufacturer worldwide.
- **Components**: ICs from Mouser; 100×150×3 mm acrylic from eBay (pre-cut); screws, standoffs (3D-printed or commercial).
- **No soldering required**: firmware loaded by BOOTSEL+copy UF2; ICs inserted into sockets with orientation matching notches; acrylic case stacked with bolts.
- **Common mistake demonstrated**: IC inserted backwards — must match notch on socket/silkscreen.
- **Board giveaway**: one assembled board given to a random commenter; empty PCBs for every 100 comments.
- **Practical note**: header pins on Pi Pico must be pre-soldered; boards from PCBWay came with headers installed.
